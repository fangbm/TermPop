The Transformer is a network architecture based entirely on attention mechanisms, dispensing with recurrence and convolutions. Experiments on machine translation tasks show that these models are more parallelizable and can achieve strong BLEU scores on WMT 2014 English-to-German and English-to-French benchmarks.

Recurrent neural networks, convolutional neural networks, encoder-decoder models, multi-head attention, positional encoding, scaled dot-product attention, residual connections, layer normalization, and softmax are all important terms in this context. The paper also discusses GPUs, TPUs, sequence transduction, self-attention, and auto-regressive decoding.

A good LLM detector should prefer terms such as RNN, CNN, BLEU, WMT 2014, Transformer, multi-head attention, positional encoding, self-attention, sequence transduction, encoder-decoder, and scaled dot-product attention. It should avoid generic words such as task, data, result, model, English, French, and German unless they are part of a longer technical phrase.
